Water Sources and Conservation Schemes.

It is essential to maintain the balance of water cycle. From the charts data, it is evident that there is not enough condensed vapour available to form rain and the reduction in water bodies is breaking the evaporation process. Following are some of the schemes we can follow to conserve water and thereby restore the water cycle balance.

What is Water Cycle?

EvaporationThe sun heat changes the water in liquid form to vapour.

Condensation: As water vapour cools down, it condenses back into tiny droplets of liquid water. This water will be weightless to float and groups together to form a cloud.

Precipitation: As more water droplets forms and clouds joins, the water droplets will become too heavy to stay in the air and will fail to earth as rain, hail or snow.

Recycled Water Schemes

Recycled water is water that is processed for all purposes other than drinking consumption. There are many ways to recycle water, which can be used for irrigation of parks, sporting fields and crops and supplying dual recticulation systems. Queensland Government currently asissts in registering providers who are avaialble in market to supply recycled water.
